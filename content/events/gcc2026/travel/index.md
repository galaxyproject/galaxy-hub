# About Clermont-Ferrand

## Clermont-Ferrand: A City of History, Innovation, and Vibrant Culture

Perched on a hill and encircled by ancient ramparts, **Clermont-Ferrand** seamlessly blends its **medieval charm** with a dynamic, modern identity. As the historic capital of Auvergne, this **compact, walkable city** of around 145,000 people (300,000 in the metropolitan area) captivates visitors with its **rich heritage** and **innovative spirit**.

Here, **UNESCO-listed treasures** like the **Romanesque Basilica of Notre-Dame-du-Port** stand alongside **Renaissance palaces**, ornate fountains, and **Art Nouveau facades**, creating a visually stunning cityscape. But Clermont-Ferrand isn't just about history—it's also the **birthplace of [Michelin](https://www.michelin.com/en/)**, a global leader in tire manufacturing, and a thriving hub for **technology and research**.

The city's lively atmosphere is fueled by its **student population**, filling the streets with energy, creativity, and a bustling cultural scene. From **cafés and theaters** to **music venues and museums**, Clermont-Ferrand offers a **diverse array of experiences**—whether you're exploring its **architectural gems**, savoring its **gastronomic delights**, or enjoying its **vibrant nightlife**.

For more inspiration, check out our **[Local Attractions](#local-attractions)** section below!

## Weather in Late June

Late June in **Clermont-Ferrand** brings **ideal summer conditions**—warm, sunny. With **daytime temperatures averaging 20°C to 26°C (68°F to 79°F)**, you'll enjoy comfortable weather for strolling through the city's historic streets or exploring the stunning **Auvergne countryside**.

While the days are mostly **sunny and pleasant**, the **Massif Central's microclimate** can occasionally bring **brief afternoon thunderstorms**. Pack **light layers, comfortable walking shoes, and a compact umbrella or light jacket** to stay prepared. Don't forget **sun protection**—sunscreen, a hat, or sunglasses—to make the most of your time outdoors.

One of the highlights of summer in Clermont-Ferrand is the **long daylight hours**, with the sun setting around **9:30 PM**. This gives you **plenty of time** to enjoy evening walks, al fresco dining, or a relaxed drink at a café after the conference sessions. Whether you're discovering the city's cultural gems or unwinding in its lively atmosphere, the weather in late June sets the stage for an unforgettable visit.

# Getting to Clermont-Ferrand

## By Air

The **Clermont-Ferrand Auvergne Airport (CFE)**, just a **15-minute drive** from the city center, offers **daily flights to Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG)**. From the airport, you can reach downtown in **20 minutes** by taking **bus line 20** toward **GERZAT Champfleuri**. Tickets cost **€1.60** and can be purchased at the airport’s vending machine or directly on the bus with a credit card. **Pro tip:** Buses are **free on weekends**!

For **international travelers**, **Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG)** and **Lyon–Saint Exupéry (LYS)** airports provide the best global connections. **CDG** offers **three daily direct flights** to Clermont-Ferrand, while **LYS** ensures smooth onward travel via **train, rental car, or shuttle bus**, giving you flexibility for your journey. Whether by air or land, reaching Clermont-Ferrand is simple and convenient.

## By Train

**Intercités trains** whisk you from **Paris Gare de Bercy** to the heart of Auvergne in just **3.5 hours**, with multiple daily departures. For those coming from **Lyon**, direct trains take only **2 hours**, offering a quick and comfortable journey.

Upon arrival, **Clermont-Ferrand's centrally located train station** provides seamless access to the city's **public transportation network**, making it simple to start exploring right away. Whether for business or leisure, traveling by train is a **relaxing and scenic** way to experience the beauty of central France.

## By Car

Clermont-Ferrand is **perfectly positioned** for those traveling by car, with **direct access via the A71, A72, and A75 motorways**. The **A75** stands out as a **toll-free highway**, offering a **picturesque route** that stretches all the way to the **Mediterranean coast**. Whether you're arriving from cities across France or neighboring countries, these **well-maintained highways** make road trips a **convenient and scenic** choice, allowing you to enjoy the stunning landscapes of the Auvergne region along the way.

The iconic **Puy de Dôme** volcano dominates the skyline **just 15 km west of the city** and is an absolute must-see. At **1,465 meters**, this dormant volcano offers **spectacular panoramic views** of the entire **Chaîne des Puys volcanic range** and the surrounding countryside. You can reach the summit effortlessly aboard the **Panoramique des Dômes train** — a scenic **15-minute ride** — or hike up one of several trails.

The Puy de Dôme is a **UNESCO World Heritage site** and home to ancient **Roman temple ruins** and modern communications antennas. On clear days, the **360-degree views** are breathtaking and provide excellent photo opportunities.

To reach the Puy de Dôme it's around 25min from the city center or there's shuttle bus called ["La navette du Panoramique des Dômes"](https://www.panoramiquedesdomes.fr/comment-venir/) (free on weekends) for a convenient and eco-friendly option.

# Culinary Scene

## Regional Gastronomy

The **Auvergne region** is a paradise for food lovers, celebrated for its **hearty, rustic cuisine** shaped by its volcanic landscapes and rich farming heritage. At the heart of its gastronomy are **world-class cheeses**—**Saint-Nectaire, Bleu d’Auvergne, Cantal, Fourme d’Ambert, and Salers**—each crafted locally and adding depth to traditional dishes.

Must-try specialties include:
- ***Truffade***: A comforting blend of potatoes and melted cheese.
- ***Potée auvergnate***: A robust stew brimming with local meats and vegetables.
- ***Pounti***: A unique savory cake made with Swiss chard and prunes.
- ***Aligot***: A creamy, elastic blend of mashed potatoes and melted cheese, a true Auvergne icon.

No visit is complete without sampling **Puy lentils**, a prized local delicacy renowned for their **nutty flavor and firm texture**. Whether you're indulging in a rustic meal or exploring gourmet creations, Auvergne's cuisine promises an unforgettable taste of its volcanic terroir.

## Dining in Clermont-Ferrand

Clermont-Ferrand boasts a dynamic and diverse dining scene, where tradition and innovation coexist harmoniously. From charming **traditional restaurants** serving hearty regional specialties to **cutting-edge contemporary eateries**, there’s something to satisfy every palate.

The city’s lively **student population** fuels a thriving café culture, offering an array of **affordable bistros** and global flavors—perfect for casual meals or social gatherings.

For a true taste of local life, explore the bustling **Saint-Pierre market**, where the region's finest produce, artisanal cheeses, cured meats, and wines take center stage. The volcanic soil of the surrounding area gives rise to the unique **Côtes d’Auvergne wines**, which pair wonderfully with the area's rustic dishes.

No visit is complete without savoring a glass of **verveine du Velay**, the region's iconic green liqueur, or sampling one of the **exceptional craft beers** brewed in Auvergne—a delightful way to experience the local terroir.

# GCC2026 Host: Université Clermont Auvergne

## About the University

**GCC2026 will be hosted by [Université Clermont Auvergne (UCA)](https://www.uca.fr/en)**, a comprehensive research university that was formed in 2017 through the merger of two major institutions. With **over 37,000 students and 3,500 staff members**, UCA is one of the largest universities in France and a key driver of innovation in the region.

The university excels in numerous fields including **physics, chemistry, health sciences, and computer science**. It operates several cutting-edge research facilities and maintains strong partnerships with industry, particularly in the **technology and engineering** sectors. UCA is actively engaged in **bioinformatics and computational biology** research, especially via the [**Auvergne Bioinformatics** facility](https://mesocentre.uca.fr/projets-associes/plateforme-aubi), making it an ideal host for the Galaxy Community Conference.

# GCC2026 Location

**GCC2026 will be held at the [IUT Clermont Auvergne](https://iut.clermont.auvergne.fr/)**—a branch of **Université Clermont Auvergne (UCA)** specializing in **technical and professional education**.
