# Galaxy Ecology: From Biodiversity data handling and analysis to Essential Biodiversity Variables (EBV)

Since 2017, French Museum of Natural History is leading the Galaxy Ecology initiative to test the interest of using the Galaxy platform for the need of Biodiversity data oriented communities. After several years developping tools, workflows and tutorials, to deal with highly heterogeneous data as GIS, eDNA, species occurences or remote sensing, we finally published in 2025 our fundamental article "Guidance framework to apply best practices in ecological data analysis: lessons learned from building Galaxy-Ecology" (Royaux et al. 2025,GigaScience, Volume 14, 2025, giae122, https://doi.org/10.1093/gigascience/giae122). In this article, we described our "atomization / generalization framework" specially adapted to enhance FAIRness of ecological data analysis pocesses. Since 2017, we were also notably challenging the use of Galaxy to facilitate the sharing of computational workflows to produce biodiveristy metrics and indicators through a GEO BON EBV (Essential Biodiversity Variable) operationalization pilot
